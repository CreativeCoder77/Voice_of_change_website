Equality Towards Backward Classes in India

Read More

↓

Constitutional Provisions

  • Article 15: Prohibits discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, gender, or place of birth. Allows special provisions for socially and educationally backward classes.
  • Article 16: Provides reservation in public appointments and promotions for SCs, STs, and OBCs. The 103rd Constitutional Amendment (2019) introduced a 10% reservation for economically weaker sections (EWS).

Commissions and Reports

  • Kaka Kalelkar Commission (1953): The first commission to identify backward classes and recommend measures for their advancement.
  • Mandal Commission (1978): Defined "socially and educationally backward classes" and recommended a 27% reservation in government jobs for OBCs. It identified 3,743 castes as OBCs.

Legislative Measures

  • Prevention of Atrocities Act (1989): Enacted to protect SCs and STs from discrimination and violence, ensuring their right to live with dignity.
  • Supreme Court Judgments: Landmark cases like Indra Sawhney (1992) upheld the reservation policy, establishing that total reservations should not exceed 50% and introducing the "creamy layer" concept.

Implementation of Reservations

  • Reservation Quotas: Currently set at 15% for SCs, 7.5% for STs, and 27% for OBCs in government jobs, adjusted based on state demographics.
  • Promotion Reservations: The M. Nagaraj v. Union of India (2006) case upheld promotions for SC/ST employees if underrepresented in public services.

Recent Developments

  • Economic Reservation: The 103rd Amendment introduced an additional 10% reservation for economically weaker sections (EWS) among the general category, expanding opportunities for those from economically disadvantaged backgrounds.
