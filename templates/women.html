Steps taken by Government to Empower women

Read

↓

1950s

Building Foundations of Equality

  • Constitutional Rights for Women: Ensured equal rights and non-discrimination through Articles 14 and 15.
  • Hindu Marriage Act (1955): Gave women legal rights in marriage and divorce.
  • Women's Participation: Early steps to increase women's roles in governance and public institutions.
2006-2010

Legal Protections and Women's Safety

  • Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act (2005): Provided protection for women against domestic violence.
  • National Commission for Women: Strengthened to advocate for women's rights and monitor issues of gender equality.
  • Gender Sensitization Programs: Implemented in schools and workplaces to promote a safer environment for women.
2011-2015

Women Empowerment and Education

  • Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (2015): Aimed at improving the child sex ratio and promoting girl education.
  • Digital Literacy for Women: Introduced programs to educate women on digital literacy and entrepreneurship.
  • Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (2015): Launched to financially empower girls and encourage saving for their education.
2016-2020

Economic Empowerment and Safety

  • Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act (2017): Extended maternity leave from 12 weeks to 26 weeks.
  • Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (2017): Launched to provide maternity benefits and nutritional support.
  • Women's Self Help Groups (SHGs): Strengthened as part of the National Rural Livelihood Mission to increase economic independence.
2021-2023

Health and Digital Empowerment

  • National Digital Health Mission: Created a digital health ecosystem to provide secure and accessible healthcare data for women.
  • Mission Shakti: Launched to integrate protection, safety, and empowerment services for women under one umbrella.
  • Expansion of One-Stop Centres: Established for women affected by violence, offering medical, legal, and psychological support.
2024-2029

Scaling Empowerment Through Technology

  • Digital India for Women: Enhanced focus on digital literacy for women in rural and urban areas, promoting entrepreneurship and e-governance.
  • AI and Women’s Safety: Using AI and technology to improve women's safety and streamline services like helplines and tracking.
  • Green and Inclusive Workforce: Training women for roles in renewable energy and technology-driven sectors.
2030-2035

Transforming Women's Health and Education

  • Healthcare for All Women: Universal access to healthcare services, with special focus on maternal health and reproductive rights.
  • Complete Digitization of Education: STEM and vocational training for women, aimed at preparing them for a knowledge-driven economy.
  • AI in Governance: Using AI tools to monitor and improve women's representation and participation in policy-making and governance.
2036-2040

Sustainable Development for Women

  • Environmental Leadership: Women’s participation in global environmental restoration and conservation projects.
  • Global Technology Hub: Women leading India’s efforts as a global leader in AI, robotics, and green technologies.
  • Global Collaborations: Strengthened international partnerships to enhance women’s roles in global trade, technology, and innovation.
2041-2047

Realizing Women’s Leadership in a Viksit Bharat

  • Inclusive Prosperity: Achieving an inclusive India where every woman has access to education, healthcare, and economic opportunities.
  • Leadership in Green Bharat: Women driving India’s carbon-neutral initiatives and sustainable development goals.
  • Women-Led Innovation: Ensuring women’s active participation in India’s global leadership across technology, environment, and governance sectors.
